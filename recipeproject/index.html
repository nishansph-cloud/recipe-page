Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Roman pasta made with eggs, cheese, pancetta and black pepper. Rich, creamy and ready in 30 minutes.

Prep Time

10 min

Cook Time

20 min

Servings

2 people

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

Pasta

200g spaghetti

1 tsp salt (for water)

Sauce

150g pancetta or guanciale

3 large egg yolks

1 whole egg

60g Pecorino Romano

40g Parmesan

Black pepper, generous amount

To Serve

Extra Parmesan

Fresh black pepper

Fresh parsley (optional)

Instructions

Boil The Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente — usually 1 minute less than the packet says. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.

1

Cook the pancetta

While the pasta cooks, fry the pancetta in a large pan over medium heat until crispy and golden. Turn off the heat and let it cool slightly — this is important, a hot pan will scramble your eggs.

2

Make the egg mixture

In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, whole egg, Pecorino, and Parmesan. Add a generous amount of black pepper. The mixture should be thick and pale yellow.

3

Combine everything

Add the hot drained pasta to the pan with pancetta. Remove from heat entirely. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta and toss quickly, adding pasta water a splash at a time until you get a silky, creamy sauce.

4

Serve immediately

Plate the pasta, top with extra Parmesan, a crack of black pepper, and parsley if using. Carbonara waits for no one — eat it straight away.

5

Chef's Tip

The number one mistake is adding the egg mixture to a hot pan — it scrambles instantly. Always take the pan fully off the heat and let it cool for 30 seconds before adding the eggs.
